How the Impeachment Process Works

As President Trump faces the threat of impeachment again, questions arise as to how the process works.

What is Impeachment?

Impeachment was set forth in the Constitution to give Congress the power to remove a president from office before their term has ended. The Constitution cites specific actions for impeachment and removal from office. However, only two presidents, Andrew Johnson (1868) and Bill Clinton (1998), have ever been impeached and neither were removed from office.


What Causes Impeachment?

According the Constitution, "treason, bribery, or other high crimes and misdemeanors" are impeachable offenses. High crimes and misdemeanors essentially means the abuse of power by a high-level public official. It does not necessarily have to be a violation of an ordinary criminal statute.
